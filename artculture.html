Parisian Museums and Culture

Discover Paris' culture and museums

  • The Louvre

    The world’s largest art museum, home to iconic masterpieces like the Mona Lisa and Venus de Milo, housed in a former royal palace.

  • The Pompidou Centre

    A modern art museum known for its radical architecture, with exposed pipes and bold colors, showcasing works by Picasso, Kandinsky, and more.

  • Musée d'Orsay

    Set in a Beaux-Arts railway station, this museum houses a world-renowned collection of Impressionist and Post-Impressionist masterpieces.

  • Arc de Triomphe

    A monumental arch honoring France’s military victories, featuring intricate sculptures and the Tomb of the Unknown Soldier beneath it.

  • Musée de l'Orangerie

    Famous for Monet’s massive Water Lilies murals, this museum also holds works by Cézanne, Matisse, and other modernist artists.

  • MoMA Paris (Atelier des Lumières)

    A digital art space bringing masterpieces to life through immersive projections—an innovative blend of art and technology.

  • Catacombs of Paris

    An underground ossuary holding the remains of over six million people, offering a haunting glimpse into Paris’s hidden history.
