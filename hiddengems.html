Parisian Itinerary: Hidden Gems

Discover some of Paris' lesser known attractions with this two-day itinerary

🗓 Day 1

☕️ Breakfast - Treize au Jardin

Begin your day at Treize au Jardin, a charming hidden café tucked just across from the Luxembourg Gardens. With mismatched furniture, a homey atmosphere, and a Southern-inspired brunch menu (think biscuits, grits, and banana cake), it offers a refreshing twist on the classic Parisian breakfast. The terrace looks out over the gardens, making it a perfect peaceful start to your day.

  • Recommended Duration: 45–60 minutes
  • Travel Time to next stop: 10 mins walk to Musée Zadkine

🌞 Morning - Musée Zadkine

Hidden behind lush greenery near the Luxembourg Gardens, Musée Zadkine is a quiet gem dedicated to Russian-born sculptor Ossip Zadkine. The museum blends indoor and outdoor spaces with expressive modern sculptures nestled in a peaceful garden. It’s rarely crowded, making it perfect for a slow, reflective visit far from the city’s buzz.

  • Recommended Duration: 45–60 minutes
  • Travel Time to next stop: 20 mins by Metro to Parc des Buttes-Chaumont

🍽 Lunch + Afternoon - Parc des Buttes-Chaumont & Pavillon Puebla

Spend a leisurely few hours in the lush, hilly expanse of Parc des Buttes-Chaumont, one of Paris’s most scenic and underappreciated parks. Enjoy a picnic on the sloping lawns with views of the lake and suspension bridge. Then wander to Pavillon Puebla, a romantic, bohemian hideaway nestled within the park. Surrounded by trees and glowing string lights, it’s perfect for an espresso or spritz on the leafy terrace — a magical pocket of Parisian calm.

  • Recommended Duration: 2–2.5 hours total
  • Travel Time to next stop: 25 mins by Metro to Galerie Vivienne

Afternoon –La REcyclerie

Step off the tourist path and into La REcyclerie, a former train station transformed into an eco-conscious café, urban farm, and creative hub. Perched along the old Petite Ceinture railway, this vibrant space is part repair workshop, part garden, and part community gathering place. Sip an organic drink on the terrace, wander past beehives and chickens, or browse quirky upcycled art — it’s a refreshing change from museums and manicured parks. Surrounded by locals fixing bikes or tending herbs, you'll see a grassroots side of Paris that's lively, purposeful, and completely unexpected.

  • Recommended Duration: 1–1.5 hours
  • Travel Time to next stop: 30 mins by Metro to Galerie Vivienne

🌇 Evening - Galerie Vivienne + Dinner nearby

Wind down your day at Galerie Vivienne, one of Paris’s most beautiful covered passages. With mosaic-tiled floors, antique storefronts, and soft lighting, it’s an enchanting place to window-shop or grab a quiet aperitif. For dinner, try Bistrot Vivienne just beside the arcade for an atmospheric, old-Paris meal.

  • Recommended Duration: 1.5–2 hours (including dinner)
  • Travel Time to next stop: 15 mins walk to Rue Montorgueil

🌙 Night - Rue Montorgueil stroll

Cap off the evening with a slow stroll down Rue Montorgueil — a lively pedestrian street filled with bakeries, cafés, and glowing storefronts. While popular with locals, it remains under many tourists' radar at night. Stop for a digestif or late dessert at Stohrer, one of the city’s oldest patisseries.

  • Recommended Duration: 45–60 minutes

🗓 Day 2

☕️ Breakfast - La Fontaine de Belleville

Begin your day at La Fontaine de Belleville, a vintage-style café in a lesser-explored neighborhood. Known for quality coffee and old-school charm, it feels like a slice of Paris from another era. The Belleville area has a creative, multicultural vibe that's perfect for early-day exploration.

  • Recommended Duration: 45 minutes
  • Travel Time to next stop: 10 mins walk to Canal Saint-Martin

🌞 Morning - Walk along Canal Saint-Martin

Meander along the tree-lined Canal Saint-Martin, a peaceful escape with iron footbridges, street art, and reflections on the water. It’s a slower, more authentic slice of Paris life. Grab a pastry or simply enjoy the calm atmosphere and scenic walk.

  • Recommended Duration: 1–1.5 hours
  • Travel Time to next stop: 20 mins by Metro to Musée de la Chasse et de la Nature

🍽 Lunch - Marais + Musée de la Chasse et de la Nature

Stop in the Marais for lunch at a tucked-away café like L’Éclair de Génie (for gourmet éclairs) or La Petite Place. Afterward, visit the Musée de la Chasse et de la Nature — a whimsical museum dedicated to hunting and nature with surreal art displays and ornate rooms. It’s unexpected and endlessly intriguing.

  • Recommended Duration: 2 hours (lunch + museum)
  • Travel Time to next stop: 10 mins walk to Rue Crémieux

🏛 Afternoon - Rue Crémieux

Snap a few dreamy photos on Rue Crémieux, Paris’s most colorful residential street. With pastel-painted facades and flowerpots lining the walkway, it’s a photographer’s dream. Quiet and pedestrian-only, it’s a charming interlude and a chance to slow down.

  • Recommended Duration: 30–45 minutes
  • Travel Time to next stop: 20 mins by Metro to Terrass'' Hotel Rooftop

🌇 Evening - Rooftop drinks at Terrass'' Hotel

Head to the rooftop bar of Terrass'' Hotel in Montmartre for panoramic views across Paris. This lesser-known spot offers elegant cocktails and an unbeatable vantage point of the Eiffel Tower at sunset. It's the perfect hidden-view moment to reflect on your day.

  • Recommended Duration: 1–1.5 hours
  • Travel Time to next stop: 10 mins walk to dinner

🌙 Night - Dinner at Hôtel Particulier Montmartre

End your hidden gem adventure with dinner in the secret garden of Hôtel Particulier Montmartre. This elegant townhouse is tucked behind an unmarked gate, and its lush courtyard restaurant feels like stepping into another world. Quiet, romantic, and almost entirely unknown to tourists — it’s a true final flourish.

  • Recommended Duration: 1.5–2 hours
